ENTRY THREE: "A Lesson in Patience"

Date: March 2, 2019

A transcription of a text conversation and some thoughts.

8:30 PM 2/21

(Mom is deaf.)

Mom: Hi I'm at kaiser ER they're not sure if I had a heart attack they will check on it so I may stay here overnight

C: how are you doing? is it severe?

Mom: I'm okay my heart is now okay no more racing. I'm on IV. They're doing blood work now and keeping me overnight at urgent care

C: does it hurt?

Mom: Just discomfort on my chest and back

C: do you have anyone supporting you?

Mom: No I'm here by myself but that's okay good thing I have this device to communicate. They did ekg and blood work so am waiting for results.

C: does (my sister) know?

Mom: Maybe it's a heart attack but I caught it early who knows. Yes she knows.

C: is she coming to visit?

Mom: She wanted to but told her to stay

C: i don't think it's that out of the way for her. i think it's better for you to have someone there.

Mom: Im okay for now what will she do

C: just be there for you. she doesn't need to do anything specific.

Mom: Let's wait and see what the doctor says when he has the results

10:30 PM 2/21

M: Can I talk with you on skype

C: yeah

“Mom, won’t you hug me once more? You are so far away. I don’t want you to go just yet. I know the doctor said nothing was wrong but you have now revealed to me that you have weaknesses, that you can be sick.

I am not prepared for this realization. What comes next?"

ENTRY TWO: "I'll Be Long Gone"

Date: November 19, 2017

I was on my way to school, sitting in my car at a red light. College street lights take a long time to turn green.

so the wind just started blowing.

and the trees started moving. a ton of leaves just started falling.

slowly.

all in a monochromatic orange. the guy in front of me was waving his hand out the window to a beat i couldn't hear.

i was sitting there, preoccupied by a thought about the future.

and this thought just came to me.

it wasn't dramatic or anything. it was just, "this is beautiful."

but i don't think I've had a moment these past few weeks where I've felt truly relaxed.

until that monochromatic orange.

ENTRY ONE: "Nine"

Date: April 27, 2017

Flickering eyelashes, like fireflies, caught in the heat of a thick summer night. Flashing a dim hazel, the taste of a soapy bubble mix lingered.

Her eyes waded through the air itself. Her smile peeled apart the summer heat.

She held a piece of paper between her fingers and held it like one would hold a children's drawing.

Picking detail by detail, looking for a word to say. When the only words that could be shared, were conveyed with the filling silence.

This is a site for writing, for writing about vignettes, about life, and whatever else comes to mind.
